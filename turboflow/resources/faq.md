</details>

<summary>Is TurboFlow available on mobile?</summary>

Yes. TurboFlow is available on mobile with support for both iOS and Android. Please refer to the TurboFlow App page or [official download entry](../products/turboflow-app.md) for the latest access options.

<details>

<summary>Why hasn’t my deposit or withdrawal arrived?</summary>

Due to the uncertainty of on-chain conditions, deposits or withdrawals may occasionally take longer than usual. Under normal conditions, most transactions are completed within a few minutes. If your transaction remains delayed for an extended period, please contact official support.

</details>

<details>

<summary>Why can’t I log in?</summary>

If you’re unable to log in, try switching between mobile data and Wi-Fi, disabling any network proxy, or reopening the app or webpage. App users may also try reinstalling the app. If the issue continues, please contact [official support](https://t.me/turboflow_xyz).

</details>

<details>

<summary>Why does TurboFlow sometimes feel laggy?</summary>

If you experience lag during use, try switching between mobile data and Wi-Fi, disabling any network proxy, or reopening the app or webpage. App users may also try reinstalling the app, and restarting the device if it is overheating. If the issue continues, please contact official support.

</details>

<details>

<summary>Why does order confirmation sometimes feel delayed?</summary>

All trades on TurboFlow require on-chain confirmation.

Under normal conditions, order confirmation may involve a delay of around 300–500 milliseconds, which is considered expected behavior for the platform. If delays appear significantly longer than usual, users should first check whether their local network connection is stable before retrying.

</details>

<details>

<summary>What are Points?</summary>

TurboFlow Points are part of the [platform’s points system](../incentives/points.md) and are used to reflect eligible activity across TurbFlow.

</details>

<details>

<summary>How do trading vouchers work?</summary>

TurboFlow trading vouchers are single-use promotional credits. The voucher itself cannot be withdrawn, but eligible profit generated from it may be withdrawn under platform rules. Users should always refer to the voucher details shown on the platform for validity period and usage conditions.

</details>

### Perpetual Trading

<details>

<summary>What trading models does TurboFlow support?</summary>

TurboFlow currently supports two trading models for [perpetual trading](../products/perpetual-trading.md): **Flat-Fee Model** and **Profit-Sharing Model**. The Flat-Fee Model charges trading costs upfront, while the Profit-Sharing Model does not charge fees or slippage at entry and instead applies a share only when a trade closes in profit. Users should always refer to the live product interface for the latest applicable model details and conditions.

</details>

<details>

<summary>How are trading fees charged?</summary>

TurboFlow’s  perps trading fee treatment depends on [the trading model](../products/perpetual-trading.md#trading-models) in use. Under the **Flat-Fee Model**, opening and closing fees are charged upfront when a position is opened, and no additional fee is charged again at closing. Under the **Profit-Sharing Model,** no fee or slippage is charged at entry, and the platform only takes a share if the trade closes in profit. Users should always refer to the live product interface for the latest applicable fee details.

</details>

<details>

<summary>How do funding fees work on TurboFlow?</summary>

The current funding rate for each trading pair is shown at the top of the Trading page. A positive funding rate means longs pay shorts, while a negative funding rate means shorts pay longs. Funding is settled when the countdown ends. Under the Profit-Sharing Model, the benefiting side receives 70% of the effective funding amount, while under the Flat-Fee Model, the benefiting side receives 100%.

</details>

### Event Contracts

<details>

<summary>What are Event Contracts?</summary>

[Event Contracts](../products/event-contracts.md) are TurboFlow’s short-cycle, event-based trading products.

They allow users to choose a market, select a contract duration, and take a direction — **Higher** or **Lower** — with settlement taking place at the end of a fixed time window.

</details>

<details>

<summary>What happens if an Event Contract settles flat?</summary>

If the settlement result is unchanged under platform rules, the principal is returned.

Users should always refer to the live product interface and official rules for the latest settlement behavior.

</details>

<details>

<summary>Where do Enter Price and Exit Price come from?</summary>

Event Contracts Settlement pricing is determined through TurboFlow’s oracle-based pricing system.

</details>

<details>

<summary>How is the return rate determined?</summary>

Each Event Contract displays a return rate before entry. The return rate is currently determined through platform risk management configuration and may vary depending on the market, duration, and platform conditions. Users should always rely on the live product interface for the latest displayed return rate.

</details>
